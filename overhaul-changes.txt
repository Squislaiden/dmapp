**<u>Spell Changes</u>**
Squislaiden's Overhaul necessitates changes and additional information from 5e spells for them to function smoothly.

These changes are reflected in the 5e spells list, but should not wholly affect their usage in 5e.

**Functional Changes**
- All of the Curse Spells from Grim Hollow were standardized at 6th level.
- Upcasting within spell descriptions was removed entirely. Seek outside source, or (with DM's permission), use logic to adjust damage, range, area, target count, healing, etc. on upcast.
- Attack Speed and Cast Speed are new Overhaul stats. Any additions to existing spells are likely incredibly rare, potentially only Haste.

**"Cosmetic" Changes**
- **Mana** - Spells were given a Mana Cost. Spell Level is still maintained.
- **Typing** - Spells are given a magic type: Arcane, Divine, Nature, Eldritch, and Neutral.
- **Temporary HP** - Changed to Ward and/or Ward Regen. Ward should translate back simply enough; Ward Regen might be less straightforward.
- **AC** - Is now Dodge. Some AC spells may be converted to Block. Double Block value to get original AC value.
- **Resistance** - Now has 5 stages. *1, 2, or 3* of a given Resistance grants 25%, 50%, or 75% less damage taken respectively. *Stage 4* grants nothing, and *Stage 5* is Immunity.
- **Vulnerability** - Now has 2 stages. Impairment *(-1 Resistance)* is 50% more damage taken from type. Vulnerable *(-2 Resistance)* is double damage taken from type.
- **Exhaustion** - Converted to Malaise. Should be a 1-to-1 conversion. Many spells involving death, healing, or resurrection may have added Malaise effects.
- **Poisoned** - Remained *Debilitated* as Poison is a status effect in the overhaul.
